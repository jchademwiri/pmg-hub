# 5. Credit Notes

> A credit note reduces what a client owes.

## When To Use A Credit Note

Use a credit note when:

- A client was overcharged
- Work was cancelled after invoicing
- A discount or adjustment is approved
- Part of an invoice must be written off

Do not delete invoice history just because the amount changed. Use a credit note so the audit trail stays clear.

## Accounting Effect

A credit note reverses part of the invoice.

```text
DR Sales Revenue
CR Accounts Receivable
```

This reduces revenue and reduces what the client owes.

## Basic Workflow

1. Find the original invoice.
2. Confirm the adjustment amount.
3. Create the credit note.
4. Add a clear reason.
5. Save and review the client balance.

## Good Credit Note Reasons

- "Price adjustment approved by management"
- "Service cancelled before delivery"
- "Duplicate line item corrected"
- "Partial write-off approved"
